Retrieval-Augmented Generation

RAG Development Services

Retrieval-augmented generation combines the reasoning power of LLMs with your proprietary knowledge base — delivering precise, citable answers instead of hallucinated responses.

Challenges We Solve

Common AI Accuracy Challenges We Solve

These are the three problems that RAG is specifically engineered to fix.

❌

Our LLM gives confident but factually wrong answers

LLMs hallucinate — they generate plausible-sounding but incorrect responses when they don't know the answer. RAG forces the model to retrieve and cite real documents before generating a response, anchoring every answer in verified facts.

📚

The model doesn't know about our products, policies, or recent data

LLMs have a knowledge cutoff and know nothing about your specific organisation. RAG connects the model to your proprietary documents, product manuals, compliance policies, and real-time data sources — making it an expert on your business, not just the internet.

🔎

We can't audit or cite where AI responses came from

For regulated industries, AI responses need to be auditable and traceable. RAG systems can provide precise source citations — document name, section, and page — alongside every answer, creating the audit trail compliance teams require.

What We Deliver

Our RAG Development Capabilities

Six layers of RAG engineering that separate production systems from demo notebooks.

📥

Knowledge Base Ingestion & Chunking Strategies

Automated ingestion pipelines for PDFs, Word docs, HTML, databases, and APIs. Semantic chunking, hierarchical chunking, and sentence-window chunking strategies optimised for your document types to maximise retrieval relevance.

🗃️

Vector Database Design

Architecture and implementation of Pinecone, Weaviate, pgvector, or Chroma vector indexes. Schema design, namespace management, metadata filtering, and index optimisation for fast, scalable semantic search.

🔍

Hybrid Search (Dense + Sparse Retrieval)

Combining dense vector search with BM25 keyword search in a hybrid retrieval pipeline — capturing both semantic similarity and exact-match relevance. Typically improves retrieval recall by 15–25% over pure vector search alone.

📊

Re-ranking & Relevance Optimisation

Cross-encoder re-ranking with Cohere Rerank or custom re-ranking models improves the precision of top-k results before passing to the LLM. Contextual compression removes irrelevant content from retrieved chunks to reduce noise.

📎

Citation & Source Attribution Systems

Inline citations, footnotes, and source panels that show users exactly which documents each answer was drawn from — with links to original sources. Essential for regulated industries and any use case requiring auditability.

🧪

RAG Evaluation Frameworks

RAGAS and LLM-as-judge evaluation pipelines measuring context precision, context recall, answer faithfulness, and answer relevance. Automated regression suites that catch retrieval or generation quality regressions before they reach users.

How We Work

Our RAG Development Process

Six phases from document inventory to production-ready retrieval intelligence.

Document Audit & Architecture Design

Inventory your document types, volumes, update frequencies, and access control requirements. Design the ingestion pipeline, chunking strategy, embedding model, and vector database architecture.

Ingestion Pipeline Development

Build document parsers, chunking logic, metadata extraction, and embedding pipelines. Establish incremental update workflows so new documents enter the index automatically without full re-indexing.

Retrieval System Build & Tuning

Implement hybrid search, configure metadata filters, and integrate re-ranking. Run retrieval evaluation against your golden Q&A dataset to iteratively improve recall and precision before connecting to the LLM.

Generation & Prompt Pipeline

Design the full RAG prompt template, context injection strategy, citation formatting, and fallback handling for low-confidence retrievals. Build streaming response infrastructure for real-time UX.

End-to-End Evaluation

RAGAS evaluation across context precision, recall, faithfulness, and answer relevance. Build automated regression test suites. Establish production quality thresholds before go-live.

Production Deployment & Monitoring

Deploy on your cloud with full observability — retrieval latency, relevance scores, answer quality dashboards, and user feedback integration. Set up index refresh automation and ongoing quality alerts.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions About RAG Development

RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) is an AI architecture that enhances LLM responses by first retrieving relevant documents from a knowledge base, then providing those documents as context when generating an answer. Instead of relying solely on the LLM's parametric memory (what it learned during training), RAG grounds responses in your specific, current, proprietary information. This dramatically reduces hallucinations, extends the model's effective knowledge to your documents, and enables source attribution — making AI responses accurate, auditable, and trustworthy.

Use RAG when your knowledge base changes frequently (products, policies, regulations), when you need citable source attribution, when your documents are too large or numerous to fit in a context window, or when different queries require different knowledge subsets. Fine-tuning is better for adapting the model's style, tone, or reasoning behaviour — not for adding knowledge. The two approaches are complementary: fine-tune for behaviour, RAG for knowledge. Many production systems use both.

RAG reduces hallucinations through grounding: the LLM is instructed to generate answers based only on the provided retrieved documents, not its parametric memory. We additionally implement faithfulness scoring (checking whether the answer is actually supported by retrieved context), citation requirements (forcing the model to cite specific sources), confidence thresholds (triggering "I don't know" responses when retrieval quality is low), and automated RAGAS evaluation to measure and minimise faithfulness violations. Our RAG implementations typically achieve 90%+ reduction in hallucination rates versus vanilla LLM baselines.

RAG accuracy depends on retrieval quality, chunk quality, embedding model performance, and LLM reasoning ability.
