Overview

Nexus Matrix ("Nexus Matrix", "we", "us", or "our") operates the website nexusmatrix.com (the "Site"). This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our Site or engage our services.

By using our Site, you agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this policy. If you do not agree with any part of this policy, please discontinue use of our Site.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Information You Provide Directly

We collect information you voluntarily provide when you:

  • Fill out the contact or enquiry form (name, business email, phone number, company name, project details)
  • Subscribe to communications or newsletters
  • Engage us as a service provider and enter into a contract
  • Communicate with us via email or other channels

1.2 Information Collected Automatically

When you visit our Site, we automatically collect certain technical information, including:

  • IP address and approximate geographic location
  • Browser type and version, operating system
  • Pages visited, time spent, referral source, and exit pages
  • Device identifiers and session data

This information is collected via cookies and analytics tools (see Section 4).

1.3 Business Contact Information

As a B2B company, we may collect professional contact information such as business email addresses, job titles, and company names in the context of our commercial relationships.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

  • To respond to your enquiries and provide you with information about our services
  • To deliver our services and fulfil contractual obligations
  • To improve our website and user experience through analytics
  • To send relevant communications about our services where you have consented or where we have a legitimate interest
  • To comply with legal obligations and protect our legal rights
  • To prevent fraud and abuse of our website and services

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal data to third parties for their marketing purposes.

3. Cookies

Our Site uses cookies — small text files stored on your device — to enhance your experience. We use the following categories of cookies:

  • Essential cookies: Required for the Site to function (session management, security). These cannot be disabled.
  • Analytics cookies: Google Analytics 4 (GA4) collects anonymised data about how visitors use the Site (pages visited, time on page, traffic source). This helps us improve the Site. GA4 data is processed by Google LLC in accordance with their Privacy Policy.
  • Functional cookies: Remember your preferences and settings to personalise your visit.

You can control non-essential cookies through your browser settings. Note that disabling certain cookies may affect Site functionality.

4. Third-Party Services

We use the following third-party services that may process data about your visit:

  • Google Analytics 4 (GA4) — website analytics. Data is anonymised and processed under Google's Privacy Policy. We have configured GA4 with IP anonymisation enabled. Google Privacy Policy.
  • Google Fonts — we load web fonts from Google Fonts servers, which may log your IP address. Fonts are loaded from fonts.googleapis.com. Google Privacy Policy.

5. Data Retention

We retain personal data only as long as necessary for the purposes it was collected:

  • Contact form submissions: Retained for up to 3 years to follow up on enquiries and comply with legal obligations, then securely deleted.
  • Client project data: Retained for the duration of the contract plus 7 years for legal and financial record-keeping requirements.
  • Analytics data: GA4 data is retained for 14 months (configured in our GA4 account).

6. Your Rights

6.1 GDPR Rights (EEA, UK, Switzerland)

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA), United Kingdom, or Switzerland, you have the following rights under GDPR / UK GDPR:

  • Right of access — request a copy of the personal data we hold about you
  • Right to rectification — request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data
  • Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten") — request deletion of your data where there is no legitimate basis to retain it
  • Right to restriction of processing — request that we limit how we process your data
  • Right to data portability — receive your data in a machine-readable format
  • Right to object — object to processing based on legitimate interests or direct marketing
  • Right to withdraw consent — where processing is based on consent, withdraw it at any time

6.2 CCPA Rights (California Residents)

If you are a California resident, you have the following rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA):

  • Right to know — request information about the categories and specific pieces of personal information we collect and disclose
  • Right to delete — request deletion of personal information we have collected, subject to certain exceptions
  • Right to opt out of sale — we do not sell personal information, so this right is not applicable
  • Right to non-discrimination — we will not discriminate against you for exercising CCPA rights

To exercise any of the above rights, please contact us at privacy@nexusmatrix.com. We will respond within 30 days.

7. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data against unauthorised access, disclosure, alteration, or destruction. These measures include HTTPS encryption for all data in transit, restricted access controls, and regular security assessments. However, no method of transmission over the internet is 100% secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

8. International Data Transfers

Nexus Matrix operates globally and your data may be processed in countries outside your home country, including countries where data protection laws may differ. When we transfer data from the EEA or UK to third countries, we rely on appropriate safeguards such as Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) or adequacy decisions.

9. Children's Privacy

Our Site and services are directed solely at business professionals and are not intended for individuals under the age of 18. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children. If you believe we have inadvertently collected data from a minor, please contact us immediately.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically. When we make material changes, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. We encourage you to review this policy periodically. Continued use of our Site after changes constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.

11. Contact Us — Privacy Enquiries

For any questions, concerns, or to exercise your data rights, please contact our privacy team:

  • Email: privacy@nexusmatrix.com
  • Postal address: Nexus Matrix, Attn: Privacy, 123 Placeholder Street, City, Country 000000

If you are in the EEA and are not satisfied with our response, you have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority.
