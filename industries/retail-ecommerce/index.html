Common Questions

Retail & E-Commerce AI — Frequently Asked Questions

AI recommendation engines combine collaborative filtering (what similar users bought), content-based filtering (product attribute matching), and deep learning models to predict which products a specific user is most likely to purchase next. They process signals including browsing history, purchase history, real-time session behaviour, and contextual signals like time of day and device to serve personalised recommendations.

Our retail demand forecasting models typically achieve 15–30% lower MAPE (Mean Absolute Percentage Error) compared to traditional statistical forecasting methods. Accuracy depends on historical data depth, seasonality, and promotional complexity. We incorporate external signals including weather, economic indicators, social trends, and competitor pricing for best-in-class forecast accuracy.

Visual search lets shoppers find products by uploading an image instead of typing keywords. It works by extracting visual feature embeddings from product images using convolutional neural networks, then matching query image embeddings against the product catalogue using approximate nearest-neighbour search. We build visual search systems that handle millions of products with sub-second response times.

Yes. Our generative AI product content systems can produce SEO-optimised product titles, descriptions, bullet points, and meta tags from product attributes, images, and category context. This is particularly valuable for marketplaces with large catalogues — we've helped retailers generate content for 500,000+ SKUs with consistent brand voice and quality.

Dynamic pricing AI continuously optimises prices based on demand signals, competitor pricing, inventory levels, customer segment, time of day, and elasticity models. The system learns price-demand relationships from historical transaction data and applies reinforcement learning or optimisation algorithms to maximise revenue or margin subject to business rules and competitive positioning constraints.

A recommendation engine needs at minimum: product catalogue data (attributes, categories, images), user interaction data (views, clicks, add-to-cart, purchases), and transaction history. More signals — search queries, reviews, wishlists, session sequences — improve recommendations. Cold-start for new users is handled with content-based and popularity-based fallbacks.

A recommendation engine or demand forecasting MVP typically takes 8–12 weeks from data access to first production deployment. Full personalisation platform implementations with A/B testing infrastructure, real-time scoring APIs, and analytics dashboards typically require 16–24 weeks. We use phased delivery so you see value early.
